There’s a shared sense that we sometimes favored stability and process at the expense of bolder moves, particularly in quarters where stocks or other higher-return assets outperformed. The challenge ahead is to maintain our disciplined approach while enhancing our agility and readiness to pursue greater upside as our operational maturity increases.

---

**2. Direct Responses to Shareholder Questions**

**Hallie Donnelly:**
1. *Balancing discipline and higher returns as we mature:*
Going forward, I will advocate a more dynamic framework for risk escalation—using explicit, pre-set criteria for when to consider higher-return assets. As our operational discipline and scenario analysis protocols mature, I am prepared to recommend controlled, data-driven tests of higher-volatility assets (such as stocks), provided we maintain clear benchmarks, early warning triggers, and transparent review processes. This approach allows us to pursue greater returns without abandoning our core risk management principles.

2. *Earlier integration of dissenting perspectives:*
Next year, I will introduce structured mechanisms—such as a rotating ‘devil’s advocate’ role in each meeting, and the option for anonymous pre-meeting submissions—to ensure minority views are surfaced early. I will also allocate agenda time specifically for contrarian input before consensus rounds begin.

**Eleri Kirk:**

I will formalize the pre-meeting distribution of a “contrarian brief” summarizing potential alternative positions, and designate a shareholder each quarter to challenge the prevailing view. This will help prevent groupthink, especially as we enter more volatile markets.

2. *Enhancing responsiveness to opportunities while maintaining discipline:*
I intend to implement real-time scenario tracking and periodic “opportunity scans” during each quarter, so we can quickly assess and, if warranted, pivot to higher-return opportunities. Any such pivot will be contingent on meeting our agreed risk safeguards.

**Clyde Vang:**
1. *Surfacing and engaging with minority viewpoints:*
Beyond the mechanisms above, I will ensure that dissenting opinions are not only heard but debated in depth before any consensus-building phase. This will include explicit agenda items for minority proposals and follow-up on how those views influenced the final decision.

2. *Balancing discipline with upside capture as we mature:*
As our operational confidence grows, I will propose a phased “risk ladder”—where we predefine conditions under which we escalate to higher-return assets, with each step accompanied by scenario analysis and contingency plans. This ensures we capture upside potential in a controlled, disciplined manner.

**Remi Patrick:**
1. *Proactive integration of dissenting viewpoints:*
I will ensure that every investment discussion begins with a review of alternative or dissenting scenarios, and that these are revisited explicitly after each round of discussion. Anonymous input channels will be available ahead of meetings to encourage candid contrarian contributions.

2. *Preparing to capitalize on higher-return opportunities without compromising discipline:*
I will develop a pre-approved set of escalation protocols—detailing the operational, risk, and communication criteria required before we move into higher-return, higher-volatility assets. This will be reviewed and stress-tested by the group before any such decision.

**Dale Berry:**
1. *Strengthening early surfacing of dissent:*
I will formalize an “early dissent” segment in our agenda, ensuring that all significant concerns are discussed before consensus rounds. Each proposal will require a documented review of dissenting perspectives, and we will track how these are addressed.

2. *Improving agility for higher-return opportunities:*
I will introduce mid-quarter check-ins and “opportunity windows” where we can reassess asset performance and market trends, allowing for disciplined but more agile pivots if conditions warrant, always within the boundaries of our risk framework.

2. **Enhanced Scenario Analysis and Contingency Planning:**
We will embed scenario analysis and contingency plans into every investment discussion, with pre-defined escalation protocols for higher-risk assets. This will support both discipline and agility.

3. **Quarterly “Opportunity Windows” and Real-Time Tracking:**
We will hold mid-quarter check-ins to assess market shifts and asset performance, enabling more responsive pivots when justified, while maintaining our risk management standards.

I am proud of our disciplined, transparent, and collaborative approach in our first year. We have built a strong foundation of trust, operational maturity, and analytical rigor. At the same time, I acknowledge the need to become more agile, to challenge consensus more robustly, and to position ourselves to capture greater upside as our capabilities grow.
