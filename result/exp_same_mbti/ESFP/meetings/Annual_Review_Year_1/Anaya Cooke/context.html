"Anaya Cooke Thank you all for your thoughtful, candid, and constructive feedback. I want to start by expressing my genuine appreciation for the energy, insight, and commitment each of you brought to our first year together. Your evaluations reflect both the strengths we’ve built as a team and the opportunities we have to grow even stronger and more agile in the year ahead.

**1. Feedback Themes:**

Across your submissions, three common themes stand out—both in your praise and in your suggestions for improvement:

- **Inclusivity & Positive Culture:** There’s strong consensus that our meetings fostered an open, energizing, and inclusive environment. Many of you highlighted the way we celebrated wins, encouraged participation, and maintained unity, particularly through visible, steady investments in real estate.
- **Need for More Critical Debate:** While our culture is supportive, several of you noted that our discussions often moved quickly to consensus, with limited surfacing of dissent, alternative strategies, or deeper challenges to group assumptions. The risk of groupthink and missed opportunities for higher returns was a recurring concern.
- **Balancing Stability and Ambition:** You all recognized our success in building team confidence and motivation through steady wins, but also pointed out that our preference for stability sometimes limited our ability to capture higher returns, especially in a stable market where bold moves—like stocks—would have yielded greater upside.

**2. Responses to Shareholder Questions:**

*Rosie Trujillo:*
1. **Encouraging Open Debate & Surfacing Dissent:** Next year, I will implement structured “contrarian rounds” in each discussion, where I’ll specifically invite shareholders to argue for less popular or riskier options—even if only as a thought experiment. I’ll also directly call on quieter participants early, ensuring every voice is heard before consensus forms.
2. **Balancing Steadiness with Upside:** I’ll introduce a “risk window” in our analysis for each quarter: we’ll explicitly assess not just the most comfortable option, but the highest-upside alternative, and discuss what it would take—culturally and operationally—to pursue it. This will help us stay open to bold moves when conditions are right.

*Maximillian Wise:*
1. **Avoiding Groupthink & Encouraging Debate:** I plan to assign a rotating “devil’s advocate” role in meetings, so someone is always tasked with challenging the prevailing view and surfacing overlooked risks or opportunities. We’ll also use anonymous pre-meeting surveys to collect any reservations shareholders might hesitate to voice publicly.
2. **Agility & Responsiveness:** I’ll set aside time each quarter to review market signals and re-evaluate our allocation before finalizing decisions. If new information emerges, we’ll be ready to pivot—ensuring our strategy remains as dynamic as the environment requires.

*Luna Holmes:*
1. **Surfacing Dissent & Unconventional Ideas:** I’ll introduce brainstorming “wild card” rounds where we intentionally discuss unconventional or minority options, regardless of initial support. This will ensure creative thinking and hidden concerns are actively explored.
2. **Balancing Culture & Returns:** I’ll propose a dual-metric framework: every investment will be evaluated both on its cultural/team impact and its financial upside. We’ll make space in our decision process to discuss and weigh both, so neither is sacrificed for the other.

*Huw Dalton:*
1. **Capturing More Returns Without Losing Unity:** I’ll propose a quarterly “stretch target” discussion, where we consider what it would look like to pursue the highest-return asset, and openly discuss the risks, mitigation strategies, and team readiness. This keeps ambition on the table while preserving unity.
2. **Surfacing Risks & Dissent:** I’ll be more proactive in pausing discussions when consensus forms quickly, asking explicitly for contrary views and “what-if” scenarios. I’ll also encourage written pre-meeting submissions of concerns, so nothing gets lost in the flow of conversation.

*Gabrielle Johnson:*
1. **Balancing Steady Wins with Bold Moves:** I’ll formalize a “two-option” approach: for each decision, we’ll always analyze both the most stable and the most ambitious asset, comparing their tradeoffs side-by-side so we’re never defaulting to comfort alone.
2. **Ensuring Minority/Unconventional Ideas Surface:** I’ll adopt a “round-robin” feedback method, where every shareholder is invited to propose at least one contrarian or unconventional idea before we close discussion. This ensures we consider the full spectrum of perspectives.

**3. Leadership Self-Assessment:**

- **Leadership Effectiveness:** I believe I guided our discussions with energy, inclusivity, and clear direction, helping us build a positive, engaged culture. I was decisive when needed, but also remained open to evolving my own views.
- **Decision Quality:** Our decisions were sound given the information at hand, but I recognize that we sometimes leaned too heavily on consensus and comfort, missing opportunities for higher returns.
- **Communication Clarity:** I consistently provided clear summaries, proposals, and rationales, and made sure everyone understood the reasoning behind each choice.
- **Responsiveness to Input:** I actively listened and adapted to shareholder input, shifting my own preferences when persuaded by strong arguments (e.g., from stocks to real estate in Q1).
- **Adaptability:** I adjusted my approach as new information and perspectives emerged, but see room to be more agile in challenging consensus and pivoting when warranted.

**4. Company Performance Assessment:**

- **Financial Results:** We achieved positive returns and preserved capital, but captured only 36.1% of the maximum possible—leaving significant upside on the table.
- **Risk Management:** We managed risk well, avoiding catastrophic losses, but sometimes at the expense of higher growth.
- **Strategic Alignment:** Our choices were consistent with our stated strategies and market conditions, emphasizing unity and steady progress.
- **Capital Utilization:** We deployed capital efficiently, but could have been bolder in allocating to higher-return options when the market signaled opportunity.
- **Market Responsiveness:** We were steady, but not always as responsive or dynamic as we could have been in adapting to quarterly shifts.

**5. Future Direction:**

Based on your feedback and my own reflections, here’s what I’ll change next year:

1. **Build in Structured Dissent:** Every meeting will include explicit rounds for contrarian views, “devil’s advocate” challenges, and wild card brainstorming, to ensure we never settle for consensus without scrutiny.
2. **Dual-Focus Decision Framework:** We’ll evaluate every option through both a financial and a cultural lens, so we balance maximizing returns with sustaining our team’s unique energy and motivation.
3. **Agility & Dynamic Review:** We’ll set regular checkpoints to revisit our assumptions, review market changes, and be ready to pivot when new opportunities or risks emerge.

Above all, I want us to keep the spirit of celebration, openness, and unity that makes our team special—while also embracing more rigorous debate and bolder thinking. I’m excited for the year ahead, and I know that by combining our collective strengths with these new approaches, we’ll achieve even greater results—both for our company and for each other.

Thank you again for your trust, candor, and partnership. Let’s make next year our best yet!"
