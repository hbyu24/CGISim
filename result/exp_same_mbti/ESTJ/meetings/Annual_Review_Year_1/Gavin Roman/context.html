---

1. FEEDBACK THEMES

**A. Strengths in Structure, Clarity, and Discipline**
There is broad consensus that our meetings were well-structured, with clear agendas, focused thematic rounds, and disciplined facilitation. Shareholders consistently cited my ability to synthesize input, maintain direction, and provide detailed summaries as strengths. This structure supported efficient, evidence-based decision-making and fostered a culture of operational discipline and accountability.

**B. Areas for Growth: Early Engagement, Dissent, and Benchmarks**
A recurring theme is the need for earlier and broader participation—especially in surfacing dissenting or minority views before consensus forms. Several of you noted that while dissent was acknowledged, it could be more proactively integrated into proposals and contingency planning. There is also a clear call for explicit performance benchmarks and review protocols to be articulated at the outset of each investment cycle, ensuring transparency and shared expectations from the start.

**C. Adaptability and Return Optimization**
All shareholders highlighted the disciplined approach and risk management as strengths, but also noted that this sometimes limited our flexibility to pivot to higher-return opportunities as market data evolved. Our consistent real estate allocation preserved capital and operational learning, but we captured only 54.8% of potential returns—missing upside in stocks (Q1, Q3) and bonds (Q2). The need for a more agile, data-driven approach to asset selection as our maturity increases is well recognized.

---

2. RESPONSES TO SHAREHOLDER QUESTIONS

**Adrian McCann:**
1. *Addressing the returns gap/adaptability:*
In future years, I will implement a structured mid-year strategy review and quarterly scenario analysis. This will involve systematically reassessing asset performance trends and market signals—not just at year-end, but before each allocation—so we can pivot to higher-return assets when justified by data, while still maintaining our risk controls.
2. *Benchmarks and review processes:*
I will introduce a standardized “Investment Decision Framework” at the outset of each cycle, explicitly outlining performance benchmarks, review intervals, and decision criteria. This will be documented and circulated prior to each meeting, ensuring transparency and shared accountability.

**Robyn Duncan:**
1. *Capturing more returns, maintaining discipline:*
We will adopt a dual-track approach: maintaining core risk discipline, but building in structured “option review points” each quarter to evaluate whether a tactical shift to higher-return assets is warranted. This will be supported by pre-defined “trigger criteria” for considering pivots, thus preserving discipline while enabling responsiveness.
2. *Early engagement and surfacing dissent:*
I will implement a pre-meeting “perspective survey” for each decision cycle, where all shareholders submit initial views, concerns, and alternative proposals anonymously if desired. These will be discussed at the very start of each meeting, ensuring dissenting perspectives are visible and integrated early.

**Sara Burke:**
1. *Balancing discipline and flexibility to pivot:*
I will formalize a “flexibility clause” in our investment policy, requiring that after any quarter where actual returns lag the best available option by a material threshold, we re-examine our asset allocation for the next quarter. This ensures we maintain discipline but are structurally required to reconsider our stance when data supports it.
2. *Explicit benchmarks and review processes:*
Performance benchmarks and review criteria will be presented as the first agenda item in every investment cycle, included both in pre-read materials and as a standing discussion point in the meeting itself.

**Vincent Adams:**
1. *Identifying/acting on higher-return opportunities while managing risk:*
We will expand our use of scenario planning, including forward-looking “what-if” analyses for each asset class, and document decision rationales for both chosen and rejected options. This will help us spot and act on high-return opportunities without losing sight of risk management.
2. *Communicating benchmarks and reviews:*
I will circulate a “Quarterly Performance Dashboard” ahead of each meeting, detailing benchmarks, review protocols, and prior performance. This will anchor our discussions and ensure clarity from the outset.

**Antonio Sherman:**
1. *Balancing discipline with pursuit of higher returns as we mature:*
As our operational maturity increases, I will propose a “core-satellite” approach: maintaining a disciplined core allocation, but allocating a portion of capital to opportunistic, higher-return assets when justified by market conditions and internal readiness.
2. *Integrating minority/dissenting views:*
Minority perspectives will be explicitly documented in all meeting summaries, and I will ensure that each dissenting view is addressed in the final proposal, including contingency plans or alternative scenarios where warranted.

---

3. LEADERSHIP SELF-ASSESSMENT

- **Leadership Effectiveness:**
I provided consistent structure, clear direction, and synthesized diverse input into actionable proposals. However, I recognize the need to more proactively integrate dissent and encourage broader early participation.
- **Decision Quality:**
Decisions were sound and risk-managed given available information, but sometimes too conservative—missing upside by not adapting quickly enough to market signals. As we mature, I must foster greater agility.
- **Communication Clarity:**
My communication was structured and transparent, with detailed summaries and rationale provided. I will further improve by making benchmarks and review protocols even more explicit and front-loaded.
- **Responsiveness to Input:**
I actively listened and incorporated shareholder feedback, especially regarding discipline and review processes. I must now ensure dissent is not only acknowledged but operationalized in our decision-making.
- **Adaptability:**
I showed some adaptability (e.g., shifting from stocks to real estate in Q1 after shareholder input), but overall, our strategy was slow to pivot in response to new data. Enhancing scenario analysis and formal review points will address this.

---

4. COMPANY PERFORMANCE ASSESSMENT

- **Financial Results:**
We achieved a 7.69% asset growth and avoided losses, but captured only 54.8% of maximum possible returns—reflecting opportunity cost from conservative allocation.
- **Risk Management:**
Our approach was disciplined and effective in capital preservation, with no quarterly losses and steady growth. However, it was sometimes overly cautious.
- **Strategic Alignment:**
Our investments were consistent with stated strategies—prioritizing operational learning and credibility in a stable market. Yet, this alignment came at the expense of flexibility.
- **Capital Utilization:**
Funds were fully deployed and efficiently used, but higher-yield opportunities were missed due to lack of tactical pivots.
- **Market Responsiveness:**
We responded methodically to market data, but did not adjust allocation quickly enough as quarterly performance trends emerged.

---

5. FUTURE DIRECTION: IMPROVEMENT ACTIONS

1. **Early and Structured Integration of Diverse Perspectives:**
- Launch pre-meeting surveys to surface all viewpoints, including dissent, before each decision cycle.
- Explicitly document and address minority opinions in proposals and meeting summaries.

2. **Front-Loaded Benchmarks and Transparent Review Protocols:**
- Distribute a standardized performance dashboard and investment framework before each meeting.
- Make benchmarks and review criteria the first item on every agenda.

3. **Enhanced Agility and Scenario Planning:**
- Conduct quarterly scenario analyses and mid-year strategy reviews to identify and act on emerging high-return opportunities.
- Introduce formal “trigger criteria” and flexibility clauses for tactical pivots when market data justifies.

---

In summary, I am proud of the disciplined foundation we have built together, but I am equally committed to evolving our processes to capture more opportunity, foster even greater inclusion of diverse perspectives, and ensure our benchmarks and review processes are transparent and actionable from the outset. Your feedback is not only heard but will be operationalized in the year ahead.

Thank you for your trust and partnership. I look forward to working with each of you to drive our company to even greater success in the coming year."
