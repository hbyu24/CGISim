**Responses to Specific Questions**

**From Sulayman Serrano:**


**Q1: How do you plan to improve our ability to capture higher returns in future years, especially in quarters like Q1 and Q3 where Stocks outperformed Real Estate?**
I recognize that our current approach, while disciplined and aligned with our culture, may be overly cautious in certain market conditions. Moving forward, I intend to introduce a “dynamic calibration” mechanism during quarterly meetings. This would involve explicitly evaluating whether the current asset class remains optimal given recent performance data—not just market stability, but also relative returns across asset classes. In high-return Stock quarters, we will consider partial exposure or hedging strategies, even if we maintain a primary focus on Real Estate. The goal is to preserve our core values while being more responsive to short-term opportunities.

**Q2: What specific mechanisms will you introduce to encourage earlier engagement with alternative perspectives and ensure consensus is more rigorously tested before final decisions are made?**
Starting next year, I will implement a “Challenge Round” at the beginning of each quarterly meeting. Before presenting any proposal, I’ll ask shareholders to submit one alternative viewpoint or risk scenario anonymously. These will then be discussed openly to ensure diverse perspectives are surfaced early. Additionally, I’ll designate a rotating “Devil’s Advocate” role to intentionally challenge assumptions and test the robustness of our consensus.

**Q1: How do you propose we improve our responsiveness to shifting asset performance within a stable market framework to capture more of the upside without compromising our disciplined investment strategy?**
