Across your evaluations, several clear themes emerged:

- **Strengths:**

I am encouraged that my adaptability—particularly my willingness to shift positions in response to well-reasoned shareholder input—was recognized as a positive leadership quality. - **Areas for Improvement:**
A recurring theme is the need for more proactive solicitation of alternative viewpoints and explicit scenario planning earlier in each discussion. Many of you noted that while adaptability and consensus were strengths, our process sometimes became overly risk-averse, leading to missed opportunities to capture higher returns—especially in quarters where stocks outperformed. Several of you also emphasized the importance of integrating dissenting perspectives and contingency analysis to ensure robust, resilient decisions.

**2. Responses to Shareholder Questions:**

**Md Harrell:**
1. *Balancing disciplined risk management with greater responsiveness to high-return opportunities:*
Next year, I will advocate for a more dynamic risk calibration process. This will involve explicitly evaluating not just baseline risk, but also the probability-weighted upside of each asset in current market conditions. We will establish “opportunity windows” in each meeting—dedicated moments to challenge our base case and rigorously debate whether the environment justifies a calculated increase in risk exposure, especially when market signals favor higher-return assets.

2. *Ensuring earlier and more diverse scenario exploration:*
I will introduce a formal step at the outset of each discussion where we map out at least three plausible scenarios for each asset, including both optimistic and adverse outcomes. I will assign the role of “contrarian advocate” to a rotating shareholder each quarter, ensuring that dissenting perspectives and alternative strategies are surfaced before consensus forms.

---

**Darren Roth:**
1. *Adjusting our decision-making to better capture upside while maintaining risk controls:*
We will implement a two-stage decision process: the first focused on identifying the optimal risk-adjusted return, and the second on stress-testing that choice against both our risk tolerance and recent market data. This approach will help us avoid defaulting to the most conservative option and instead weigh whether the potential upside justifies a shift in strategy.

2. *Ensuring robust exploration of alternative strategies or dissenting viewpoints:*
I will begin each meeting by explicitly requesting at least one alternative strategy from the group, regardless of initial consensus. We will document these alternatives and assess their merits systematically, incorporating scenario analysis and “what-if” stress tests as a standing agenda item.

---

**Alia Bolton:**
1. *Enhancing scenario analysis and early exploration of alternatives:*
I intend to bring forward empirical risk simulations earlier in our process, using historical and current market data to model a range of outcomes for each asset. This will help us visualize both the upside and downside more concretely and foster a more evidence-rich debate before decisions are made.

2. *Improving our ability to capture higher returns while maintaining risk management:*
I will propose a quarterly “return optimization review,” where we compare our prior decisions to actual market outcomes. This retrospective will inform our forward-looking risk appetite, ensuring we remain disciplined but not overly cautious when the data supports greater ambition.

---

**Bella Dunn:**
1. *Operationalizing earlier scenario planning and alternative viewpoints:*
I will formalize early-stage scenario planning as a required agenda item. Before we narrow our focus, we will generate a scenario matrix for each asset, capturing best, base, and worst-case outcomes. I will also invite each shareholder to articulate at least one risk or opportunity the group may be underweighting.

2. *Improving our share of available market returns while maintaining prudent risk management:*
We will introduce a “performance gap analysis” each quarter—quantifying the difference between our actual returns and the maximum possible, and discussing what changes in process or risk posture might have closed that gap, always within the bounds of our risk tolerance.

---

**Gloria Arnold:**
1. *Integrating earlier scenario planning and exploration of alternatives:*
I will ensure that scenario planning and alternative perspective exploration are embedded at the very start of each investment discussion. This will include assigning a “devil’s advocate” role to surface overlooked risks and opportunities, and requiring explicit rationale for any deviation from our established baseline.

2. *Improving responsiveness to changing market signals:*
I will establish a standing agenda item for “market signal review,” where we systematically evaluate whether new data or trends should prompt a reassessment of our asset selection, even if it means revisiting initial assumptions mid-meeting.

---
